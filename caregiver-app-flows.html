User Personas

The three people who will use this app ‚Äî design every screen for all three simultaneously

üë©
Sarah, 47
Primary Caregiver ‚Äî Lives nearby, most involved
"I'm doing most of the work and my siblings don't know what's going on. I need a way to keep everyone in the loop without writing a weekly email."
Primary Flows Daily log entries, medication management, appointment scheduling, task delegation, weekly digest review
üë®
Mark, 52
Out-of-town sibling ‚Äî Wants to help but lives far away
"I feel guilty that I can't be there. I want to know what's actually happening without having to call and ask ‚Äî I feel like I'm bothering everyone when I do."
Primary Flows "Catch me up" AI summary, log browsing, task completion (calls, insurance research), weekly digest reading
üë¥
Bob, 74
Care recipient's spouse ‚Äî Involved but not tech-savvy
"My kids set something up on my phone. I just want to see what appointments are coming up and maybe add a note when something happens. Keep it simple."
Primary Flows Status check-in, appointment viewing, simple log entry (large text, voice input), reading recent entries

The Design Tension to Solve

Sarah needs power and depth. Mark needs fast information at a glance. Bob needs simplicity and large text. The solution is a role-aware interface ‚Äî the app presents the same data but in different default views based on the user's role in the care circle. An admin sees the full dashboard. A viewer sees a simplified read-only digest view. Accessibility settings (text size, contrast) are surfaced prominently, not buried in settings.
